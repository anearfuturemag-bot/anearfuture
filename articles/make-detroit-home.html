Detroit Is Paying People to Stay

The Make Detroit Home program is giving up to $15,000 to entrepreneurs, creatives, and residents who want to plant roots here. Over 300 people have already claimed it. Here's why this matters beyond the money.

A Near Future Editorial

2026

Downtown Detroit, Michigan  ·  Aerial View

Detroit has been misread for a long time. Outsiders frame it as a city in recovery. People who actually live and build here know it's something different: a city with infrastructure, culture, momentum, and a cost of living that gives your dollar actual weight. The "Make Detroit Home" program is an acknowledgment of that reality, and a $15,000 bet that more people should be in on it.

The MoveDetroit coalition launched the program earlier this year with backing from local organizations, the mayor's office, and Dan Gilbert as honorary chair. The headline is the money: up to $15,000 in stipends for entrepreneurs, creatives, and new or returning residents, covering things like home down payments, renovations, rent, or business expenses. An additional $1,000 is available to help cover moving costs, security deposits, and even day-to-day expenses like gym memberships or meal services while people get settled.

maximum stipend per participant  ·  Make Detroit Home Program

More than 300 participants are already in. The coalition is aiming to raise $10 million this year, and Gilbert has pledged to match every dollar. That's not a small program. That's an infrastructure move.

"In Detroit, $15,000 can cover a down payment. In most major cities, it barely covers first month's rent."

That's the piece that gets lost in national coverage of this story. When Realtor.com senior analyst Hannah Jones pointed out that a $15,000 incentive goes much further in Detroit than in higher-cost markets, she was naming something real. This city is still affordable in a way that New York, Atlanta, and Los Angeles are not. The builders who get here now are getting in on something early.

ANF was built around a belief: there are people doing remarkable things right now who nobody is writing about yet. Detroit has always had those people. What it hasn't always had is this level of organized, funded investment in keeping them here and attracting more of them. Make Detroit Home isn't just a retention program. It's a recruitment campaign for the kind of people who build culture.

"This city has the grit and assets to compete with anywhere in the country for talent."

The people we feature at ANF come from cities like this. They chose to stay or chose to return because they saw something other people missed. That's what community builders do. They don't wait for the city to be finished. They understand that showing up early is part of what makes it worth showing up for.

If you're an entrepreneur, a creative, or someone who has been thinking about Detroit as a place to build, this program is a real signal. Not just from a coalition, but from a city that's decided it's done losing half its graduates to other markets. Google is here. Fifth Third Bank is here. The infrastructure is growing. The cost of living is still one of the best in the country. And now there's $15,000 available to help you make the move.

The application details are through the MoveDetroit coalition. Do your research. Talk to people who are already building here. And if this city has been on your radar, it's time to stop watching and start moving.
